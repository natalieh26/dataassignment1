Social Media Trends in the Times of a Pandemic

By: Natalie Hernandez

COVID-19 has changed the way people communicate and interact with each other. With businesses closed, people working from home, and social distancing, people have had to find new ways to stay connected and entertained. As people adapt to our new virtual world, social media is seeing a rise in engagement for users across all platforms.

People are posting and checking social media more. While others are avoiding checking. In a survey of 4,500 Influenster users, 72% said that their social media consumption increased while 43% said they have been posting more. Of the social media avoiders, 20% are avoiding Facebook while 12% are avoiding Twitter. The Influenster survey found that 42% of users did not have a preferred platform for their COVID-19 updates.
Social media users are aware of the impact of their posts. In the Influenster survey 33% of respondents indicated that during the pandemic, they have avoided posts that others might find "tone-deaf." The survey indicates that their feeds have changed from life updates and selfies, to content about the impact of the virus with 17% of people describing their newsfeeds as “overwhelming.”
The content on users timelines is different. Politics have trend in what content social media users have been digesting. With a raise in awareness on topics like race and class issues, 86% of respondents agreed or strongly agreed that they had seen a difference in the type content they see others posting on social media. The Influenster survey revealed that 43% share more information and resources related to current events, while 37% are sharing more content supporting specific causes.
People are still looking for love online during these times. As people can't meet in person, they are turning to dating apps, like Tinder and Bumble. According to Business Insider, Match Group, the company who controls over 60 percent of the dating app market,there was increase in subscribers in 2020. Match Group reported that the average number of subscribers grew 11% to 10.1 million, up from 9.1 million in 2019.
